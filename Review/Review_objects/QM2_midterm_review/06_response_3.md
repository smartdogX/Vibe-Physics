# Response 3

# Last-Minute QM2 Checklist

Use this 5-10 minutes before the exam.

## 1. Core algebra

Make sure you can write immediately:

\[
[\hat a_k,\hat a_l^\dagger]=\delta_{kl},
\qquad
[\hat a_k,\hat a_l]=0,
\qquad
[\hat a_k^\dagger,\hat a_l^\dagger]=0
\]

\[
\{\hat c_k,\hat c_l^\dagger\}=\delta_{kl},
\qquad
\{\hat c_k,\hat c_l\}=0,
\qquad
\{\hat c_k^\dagger,\hat c_l^\dagger\}=0
\]

If you hesitate here, slow down before doing any operator manipulation.

## 2. Operator order

Check that you remember:
- one-body operator = create then destroy
- fermion reordering can give a minus sign
- boson reordering does not

Canonical forms:

\[
\hat O_{1b}=\sum_{kl} o_{kl}\hat a_k^\dagger \hat a_l
\]

\[
\hat O_{1b}=\sum_{kl} o_{kl}\hat c_k^\dagger \hat c_l
\]

\[
\hat O_{2b}=\frac12\sum_{klsr} o_{klsr}\hat a_k^\dagger \hat a_l^\dagger \hat a_r \hat a_s
\]

\[
\hat O_{2b}=\frac12\sum_{klsr} o_{klsr}\hat c_k^\dagger \hat c_l^\dagger \hat c_r \hat c_s
\]

## 3. Bosons: what to not miss

- Do not miss the factor
\[
\sqrt{n}
\]
when applying
\[
\hat a_k
\quad \text{or} \quad
\hat a_k^\dagger.
\]
- Repulsive two-site Bose-Hubbard with two bosons:
ground state tends to
\[
|1,1\rangle.
\]
- Attractive case:
paired states in one well become important.
- Large filling:
remember number-phase representation and rotor/Josephson picture.

## 4. Fermions: what to not miss

- Fock-state ordering matters.
- Acting with a fermion operator can produce
\[
(-1)^{\sum_{j<k} n_j}.
\]
- Pauli exclusion:
\[
(\hat c_k^\dagger)^2=0.
\]
- Contact interaction only affects opposite-spin doublons.

## 5. Two-site Bose-Hubbard

Remember the Hamiltonian:

\[
\hat H_{2BH}
=-J(\hat a_L^\dagger \hat a_R+\hat a_R^\dagger \hat a_L)
+\frac{U}{2}
\left[
\hat n_L(\hat n_L-1)+\hat n_R(\hat n_R-1)
\right].
\]

High-yield questions:
- Which states get interaction energy
\[
U
\]
?
- What is the odd state?
- What is the even 2 x 2 block?
- What happens when
\[
U \gg J
\quad \text{or} \quad
U \ll -J
\]
?

## 6. Two-site Fermi-Hubbard

Remember the Hamiltonian:

\[
\hat H_{2FH}
=-J\sum_{\sigma=\uparrow,\downarrow}
\left(
\hat c_{L\sigma}^\dagger \hat c_{R\sigma}
+\hat c_{R\sigma}^\dagger \hat c_{L\sigma}
\right)
+U
\left(
\hat n_{L\uparrow}\hat n_{L\downarrow}
+\hat n_{R\uparrow}\hat n_{R\downarrow}
\right).
\]

Check you remember:
- obvious eigenstates:
\[
|\uparrow,\uparrow\rangle,
\qquad
|\downarrow,\downarrow\rangle
\]
with energy 0
- triplet:
\[
\frac{|\uparrow,\downarrow\rangle+|\downarrow,\uparrow\rangle}{\sqrt2}
\]
- singlet:
\[
\frac{|\uparrow,\downarrow\rangle-|\downarrow,\uparrow\rangle}{\sqrt2}
\]
- even-sector matrix:
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
U & -2J \\
-2J & 0
\end{pmatrix}
\]
- for
\[
U \gg J > 0
\]
ground state is the singlet, not the triplet

## 7. Symmetry distinctions

Be able to say these clearly:
- site inversion is not particle exchange
- singlet/triplet is spin symmetry
- even/odd is left-right parity
- particle-hole transformation is not the same thing as pseudospin ladder operators

## 8. Particle-hole symmetry

Remember:
- empty and filled sectors are related
- in the two-site model:
\[
0 \leftrightarrow 4,
\qquad
1 \leftrightarrow 3,
\qquad
2 \leftrightarrow 2
\]
- at half filling, the many-site bipartite model maps to itself
- pseudospin tower is generated by
\[
\hat \Upsilon_\pm
\]
and changes energy by
\[
\pm U
\]

## 9. Common exam mistakes

Before each calculation, check:
- Did I use commutator or anticommutator?
- Did I put creation before annihilation?
- Did I accidentally use the boson interaction term for fermions?
- Did I confuse triplet with odd parity?
- Did I forget which states feel
\[
U
\]
?
- Did I lose a minus sign by reordering fermion operators?

## 10. Ultra-short self-test

If you can answer these in under a minute, you are in good shape:

1. Write the boson and fermion canonical algebra.
2. Write the one-body operator in second quantization.
3. Which two-site Fermi-Hubbard state is the singlet?
4. Which two-site states get interaction energy?
5. Why does the triplet avoid contact interaction?
6. What does particle-hole symmetry do to particle number?

## 11. Final exam mindset

- Write the basis first.
- Mark the symmetry before diagonalizing.
- Track signs slowly for fermions.
- If stuck, ask: which states couple, which states decouple, which states feel U?
- Do not rush the first line of algebra.
