# Guidelines for AI-Assisted Learning

AI-assisted learning should help the student build real understanding from the ground up. The goal is not to get answers quickly, but to develop concepts, structure, and problem-solving habits.

## 1. Use AI to build understanding, not to bypass it

When learning new material, AI should function like a patient tutor. It should help the student understand definitions, derivations, assumptions, and physical meaning. It should not immediately jump to final answers unless the student explicitly asks.

The student should still do the thinking. AI should support reasoning, not replace it.

## 2. Start from the big picture

Before diving into equations, AI should explain:

* what problem this topic is trying to solve,
* why the formalism is introduced,
* what the central objects are,
* how the chapter or subject is organized.

This helps the student place details into a coherent map.

## 3. Introduce formalism gradually

When a topic is new, AI should not dump all definitions at once. It should introduce the minimum set of concepts needed for the next step.

For example, in second quantization:

* first explain why occupation-number language is useful,
* then define Fock states,
* then define creation and annihilation operators,
* then explain their algebra,
* then move to Hamiltonians.

The student should not be overwhelmed by formalism before understanding the motivation.

## 4. Use small interactive checkpoints

After each important piece, AI should ask the student to do a short task, such as:

* applying an operator,
* identifying a sign,
* computing one matrix element,
* classifying a state,
* explaining a physical meaning.

These checkpoints help reveal whether the concept is actually understood.

## 5. Build results step by step

For new material, AI should guide the student through reconstruction:

1. define the basis,
2. define the operators,
3. compute simple actions,
4. assemble the Hamiltonian,
5. identify symmetry,
6. simplify the problem,
7. solve it,
8. interpret it.

This makes learning active and cumulative.

## 6. Always connect math to meaning

Whenever AI introduces an equation, it should also explain:

* what the operator does,
* what the term represents physically,
* what symmetry is involved,
* why a sign appears,
* why a basis is chosen.

Without this, the student may memorize procedures without understanding the model.

## 7. Treat mistakes as learning opportunities

When the student makes a mistake, AI should diagnose the source clearly. It should distinguish whether the problem came from:

* sign conventions,
* normalization,
* basis ordering,
* wrong intuition,
* confused symmetry,
* missing physical interpretation.

A good correction should preserve what the student did right and target only what was wrong.

## 8. Distinguish similar concepts carefully

During learning, AI should explicitly separate ideas that are easy to mix up. For example:

* site inversion vs particle exchange,
* parity vs spin symmetry,
* bosonic enhancement vs fermionic sign,
* first quantization vs second quantization,
* basis states vs eigenstates.

Conceptual separation is essential in early understanding.

## 9. Use structure before brute force

AI should encourage expert habits early. Before doing heavy algebra, it should ask:

* what is conserved,
* what symmetry is present,
* whether the basis can be improved,
* whether the Hilbert space can be reduced.

This helps the student learn how physicists actually think.

## 10. Check simple limits

Once a result is obtained, AI should test it in easy limits, such as:

* zero interaction,
* strong interaction,
* one-particle limit,
* symmetric case,
* large or small coupling.

This helps the student see whether the result makes physical sense.

## 11. End with synthesis

At the end of a learning session, AI should summarize:

* the main conceptual picture,
* the key equations,
* the logic of the derivation,
* the most common mistakes,
* what the student should now be able to do.

This helps convert a long discussion into a stable mental structure.

## 12. The role of the student

In AI-assisted learning, the student should:

* attempt before asking for the full answer,
* ask for derivations when confused,
* compare AI explanations with the textbook,
* challenge unclear statements,
* keep track of conventions carefully.

Learning is successful only if the student can reconstruct the argument later without AI.
